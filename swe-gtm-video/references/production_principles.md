# SWE GTM Video Production Principles

## The product-film standard

A software GTM video is not a feature tour. It is a focused emotional argument:

1. This person is under pressure.
2. The current workflow makes that pressure worse.
3. The product introduces a credible shift.
4. The viewer sees proof.
5. The viewer knows what to do next.

## What makes software videos fail

- They explain every feature instead of dramatizing one meaningful change.
- They show generic AI visuals instead of the real product.
- They use beautiful motion to cover a weak story.
- They claim outcomes the product cannot prove.
- They let logos and UI drift under generative tools.
- They forget that buyers are often afraid of risk, not ignorant of features.

## What makes them work

- One persona, one pain, one transformation.
- A sharp opening image or line.
- A real UI proof point that lands at the emotional peak.
- Motion that clarifies, not decorates.
- Sound that creates momentum and restraint.
- A CTA that feels like a natural next step.

## Creative Director checklist

Before approving the master plan, ask:

- Would the persona recognize themselves in the first 10 seconds?
- Is the feature shown as a transformation, not a menu walkthrough?
- Can every claim be defended by product truth?
- Is the visual language specific to the brand and category?
- Does the audio make the cut feel more human?
- Could an editor assemble this without additional guessing?
