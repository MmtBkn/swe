# Visual and Motion Guide

## Software-screen composition

- Treat UI as evidence. Keep it legible and honest.
- Establish spatial context before close-up details.
- Use zooms only when the viewer needs to understand a specific action.
- Avoid rapid cuts during complex UI comprehension.
- Show before/after states when transformation is the story.

## Motion language by emotion

| Emotion | Motion language |
|---|---|
| Pressure | Tight crops, layered notifications, constrained space, subtle tremor or pulse |
| Focus | Clean alignment, centered UI, slower camera, reduced noise |
| Breakthrough | Smooth expansion, reveal, lift, or a decisive cut |
| Confidence | Stable framing, crisp overlays, restrained movement |

## Generated video visual rules

- Use abstract plates to carry emotion, not fake product demos.
- Avoid generic cyberpunk/neon/robot hand imagery unless the brand deliberately wants it.
- Avoid impossible screens, fake metrics, fake customer logos, or unreadable data.
- Use image-to-video from approved screenshots when you need a grounded software presentation.

## Overlay rules

- One idea per overlay.
- Keep type out of the UI's critical interaction path.
- Use motion to point attention, not compete with product footage.
- Leave space. Premium software videos often feel expensive because they know what not to show.
